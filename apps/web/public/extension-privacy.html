fenshot browser extension privacy policy

Last updated: July 18, 2026

The short version

The extension collects nothing, stores nothing, and sends nothing anywhere. Everything happens on your device.

What the extension does

When you click the fenshot icon, the browser takes a screenshot of the visible tab (using the activeTab permission, which is granted only by your click). A neural network bundled inside the extension reads the chess position from that screenshot, on your device. The screenshot and the position never leave your browser.

What we collect

Nothing. No analytics, no telemetry, no cookies, no accounts, no identifiers. The extension makes no network requests of its own.

External links

The result screen offers buttons that open lichess.org or coachess.app in a new tab with the recognized position in the URL. That happens only when you click those buttons, and from that point the respective site's own privacy policy applies.

Open source

The complete source code, including the recognition model and this extension, is public at github.com/scoriiu/fenshot (MIT). You can verify every claim above by reading it.
